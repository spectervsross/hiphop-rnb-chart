Hong Kong 4-Day Itinerary

First-timer edition for June: budget-friendly dim sum, beaches, markets, local food, bars and iconic harbour views, organized by neighborhood.

Trip logicOne anchor area per day, so the city stays exciting instead of exhausting.
  • Expect 30–32°C, high humidity and brutal UV.
  • A 30% rain chance usually means a short afternoon downpour.
  • Carry a compact umbrella and SPF50.

Before you start

The plan clusters sights, food and nightlife by neighborhood. It favors places locals actually use over expensive hotel bars and luxury shopping.

June strategy

  • Schedule beach, ferry and outdoor sights in the morning.
  • Use cafés, covered markets and malls during short afternoon showers.
  • Morning tanning around 9:30–11:30 is more realistic than midday.
  • Use the Octopus card for the MTR, buses, ferries and convenience stores.

Core priorities

  • Three styles of dim sum and multiple local noodle meals.
  • Repulse Bay and an optional second beach session.
  • Mong Kok, Sham Shui Po, Temple Street and Causeway Bay shopping.
  • Nightlife in LKF, Wan Chai, Knutsford Terrace and Sheung Wan.

The four-day plan

Each day has one clear geographic spine, plus a practical rain alternative.

Day 01

Central & Sheung Wan

Orientation, dim sum, old streets and happy hour

Prince Edward → Star Ferry → Sheung Wan → Central / LKF
Morning

One Dim Sum

Take the MTR to Prince Edward, Exit A. Arrive around 10:00–10:30 before the lunch queue.

  • Har gow, char siu bao, cheung fun, siu mai and turnip cake
  • Backup: Tim Ho Wan Sham Shui Po
HKD 80–120
Late morning

Star Ferry

Ride the upper deck between Central and Tsim Sha Tsui. The seven-minute crossing delivers one of the best skyline views in Asia.

About HKD 5
Afternoon

Sheung Wan walkabout

Browse Hollywood Road, Cat Street and PMQ for antiques, jade, prints, handmade accessories and local design. Ride the Mid-Levels Escalator instead of walking uphill.

Dinner

Kau Kee beef brisket noodles

Order curry beef brisket noodles on Gough Street. Cash only; shared tables are normal. Arrive before the 8:30 p.m. close.

HKD 55–70
Night

Lan Kwai Fong happy hour

Go around 7 p.m., not midnight. Brickhouse, Stockton and The Pontiac fit the relaxed, social version of LKF. Continue into SoHo only if you still have energy.

2–3 drinks: HKD 120–200
Rain plan

M+ Museum or covered Sheung Wan

Use M+ at West Kowloon or stay inside PMQ and the antique shops along Hollywood Road.

Day 02

Repulse Bay & Stanley

Tanning, market browsing and Wan Chai drinks

Central → Repulse Bay → Stanley → Wan Chai
Morning

Repulse Bay

Take bus 6 or 6X from Exchange Square. Sit upstairs on the right for coastal views. The beach has lifeguards, showers and changing rooms.

  • Bring SPF50, towel, water and sunglasses
  • Walk toward the western end for fewer crowds
Lunch

Keep it simple

Choose a casual meal at The Pulse, the beachfront McDonald’s terrace or a 7-Eleven lunch.

HKD 35–90
Afternoon

Stanley Market

Take bus 260. Shop for silk robes, watercolor paintings, fans and custom name chops. The market is covered and bargaining is expected.

Waterfront

Stanley promenade

See Blake Pier, Tin Hau Temple, Murray House and the fishing boats. Add a second swim at Stanley Main Beach if the weather holds.

Dinner

Joy Hing, Wan Chai

Order roast goose, char siu or roast pork belly on rice. The fluorescent room is basic; the food is the point.

HKD 52–70
Drinks

Wan Chai

Try Mes Amis for happy hour, Rula Bula for beer, Ciao Chow for wine or The Old Man for one more expensive cocktail.

Rain plan

Stanley Market + Maritime Museum

Shorten the beach, use the covered market, then visit the Hong Kong Maritime Museum at Central Pier 8.

Day 03

Kowloon shopping day

Markets, food crawl, local fashion and Temple Street

Mong Kok → Sham Shui Po → TST → Jordan
Late morning

Mong Kok markets

Start after 11 a.m. at Ladies’ Market and Sneaker Street. Add Flower Market Road and the Goldfish Market for distinctly Hong Kong street scenes.

  • T-shirts HKD 30–50
  • Phone cases HKD 20–40
  • Local streetwear HKD 100–300
Lunch crawl

Sham Shui Po

Combine small portions from Kung Wo Beancurd Factory, Lau Sum Kee, Hop Yik Tai and Sheung Hei.

  • Tofu pudding HKD 10–20
  • Shrimp roe noodles HKD 35–45
  • Rice noodle rolls HKD 12–18
  • Egg waffle HKD 20–25
Shopping

Apliu Street and Granville Road

Browse electronics and vintage gadgets in Sham Shui Po, then move to Granville Road for independent Hong Kong fashion and sample shops.

Optional detour

Chungking Mansions

Walk through the dense mix of guesthouses, currency shops and South Asian restaurants. Curry, naan and biryani usually cost HKD 50–70.

Evening

Temple Street Night Market

Go between 8 and 10 p.m. for lanterns, street food, fortune tellers and occasional Cantonese opera. Mido Café is the retro dinner option.

Mido set: about HKD 80
Drinks

Knutsford Terrace

A calmer, more mature alternative to LKF. Happy-hour beer is often HKD 40–50 and cocktails HKD 50–80.

Rain plan

Food + Langham Place

Keep Sham Shui Po’s indoor food stops, use Langham Place in Mong Kok, then return to Temple Street if the rain clears.

Day 04

Last shopping & farewell

Dim sum, Causeway Bay and one final night

Central → Causeway Bay → Beach or museum → TST / Sheung Wan
Morning

Tim Ho Wan, IFC

Order the baked BBQ pork buns, har gow, cheung fun and turnip cake. Write your name on the list at the door.

HKD 100–150
Late morning

Causeway Bay

Use Jardine’s Crescent, Island Beverly Centre and G.O.D. for last-minute clothing, accessories and Hong Kong-designed gifts.

Fine-weather option

Shek O Beach

Take the MTR to Shau Kei Wan and bus 9. It is smaller and more village-like than Repulse Bay.

Rain option

Museum or harbour

Choose the Hong Kong Museum of History, PMQ, covered Causeway Bay malls or a light-rain walk along the Avenue of Stars.

Dinner

Mak’s Noodle

Finish with shrimp wonton noodles in a dried-flounder broth. A dai pai dong in Sham Shui Po is the more atmospheric alternative.

HKD 45–90
Last night

Choose your ending

Bar-hop between The Pontiac, Ping Pong 129 and Dead &, return to LKF happy hour, or buy 7-Eleven beers and sit by the TST waterfront.

Practical tips

The small operational details matter in Hong Kong’s June heat.

SunscreenBuy SPF50, PA++++ at Mannings or Watsons. June UV is severe, especially on beach days.
Octopus cardStart with HKD 150: HKD 50 deposit and HKD 100 stored value. Refill at convenience stores.
CashCarry HKD 300–500 daily for markets, dai pai dong and small restaurants.
LanguageEnglish is common in Central and TST; use camera translation or point at dishes in local neighborhoods.
RainMost June showers last 20–40 minutes. Duck into a café or covered market rather than cancelling the day.
SafetyHong Kong is generally very safe for solo travelers, including at night in the listed areas. Use normal city awareness.
